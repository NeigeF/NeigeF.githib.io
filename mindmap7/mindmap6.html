Forward Modelling the Secular Evolution of the Galactic Disk

Abstract

Introduction

  • Disk Galaxies
    • are, first of all, galaxies
      • ensemble of gas, dust, stars, DM etc.
        • remember to complain about the definition
      • with very regular properties
        • supported by rotation
        • Tully-Fisher & more scaling relations
          • instead of a list of scaling relations, make 2 graphical models
            • 1. correlation graph
            • 2. graphical model with some interpretation
      • that are expected to form their disk from inside-out, but not always seen doing it
        • introduce MoMaoWhite98 model with some cosmology
        • show IFU results: not really strong mass-weighted age gradients in disk galaxies
      • that may have non-axisymmetric structures
        • I do NOT want to go the Hubble sequence way for the morphologies, I find it absolutely confusing (I agree historical background is important, but only when it brings insights or questions...)
      • that may have satellites or a funny past accretion history
    • To which extent do their present day structures reflect
      • Initial conditions (do they actually grow from inside-out?) Lz, merger history
      • Gradual memory erasing evolutionary processes
        • how important are external/internal processes?
        • do non-axisymmetries actually play a role in redistributing the stellar content?
        • where do exponential profiles come from?
    • are however difficult to study externally
      • far away form us, integrated properties, etc.
      • very low level of detail
      • no access to physical scatter
    • This thesis is by-passing these problems to address the above questions by tackling a special disk galaxy: the MW
  • The Milky Way as a model organism
    • Our galaxy
      • is sort of typical
        • mass, size, [...]
      • can be described by
        • some structural properties:
          • bar, disks, halo, central BH... (I don't like the division in 'components' but that's the easiest way to make a broad description)
          • the youngest 'component' is the low alpha disk, and it presumably formed after the last major merger. Good testbed for evolution near isolation
    • has been targeted by many surveys
      • yielding spectroscopic
        • abundances, ages, v_los, ...
          • SEGUE, APOGEE, GALAH
      • and astrometric
        • positions, pm
          • Hipparcos, Gaia...
      • and we have now enormous, multidimensional, homogeneous datasets
      • but a framework to extract information from these datasets has not been fully developed yet
        • in the literature: too many 2D plots with qualitative interpretation
        • So this thesis is about constructing a global forward model for these data
        • this is difficult
          • we do not see the MW in its entirety
          • we do not see all stars
            • quick stellar evolution reminder (e.g. young stars brighter)
          • dust makes it more difficult
            • quick explanation on extinction etc.
    • The method of this thesis to study the Milky Way
      • forward modeling
        • pluggs in a parametrized story for the Milky Way down to the dataset
        • and fits for the model parameters
      • galactic dynamics
        • define 'actions' etc.
    • We are going to implement this method incrementally through the thesis, and find-out something new about the MW each time.
      • In chapter 2, we make a simple position-feh-age model and infer how much stars have scrambled in Galactocentric radius
      • In chapter 3, we generalize to 'density modeling' and account for the spatial selection function, to infer the grow of the disk with time
      • In chapter 4, we build on a dynamical model to quantify the relative importance of heating and migration
      • In chapter 5, we use simulations to understand what the most important drivers of the secular evolution are
